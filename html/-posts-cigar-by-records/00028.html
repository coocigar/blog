Cigar Review: Cohiba Siglo I

This is a pricy cigar. For a small size with 42 Ring Gauge and 10cm in length, it costs about $42~46 Canadian dollars before tax. 💸

The band is so shiny and pretty. The construction is overall good. It is evenly packed throughout, but it's not very tightly packed. However, the draw is comfortable and slightly more on the looser side.

The first few draws of this cigar was ok. I can taste a bit of creamy sweet vanilla at the start. However, spice pretty much is the dominant taste which blocked everything. The spice was about 70% there at the start, then 80% and became 100% at the end.

I don't have much complains for the burn. It stayed pretty much straight throughout and I didn't have much issue. The ash was not the prettiest, but it built up good. 👍

After 5 minutes in, I had to change location because I didn't realize that I can't stay at a shopping area after hours. I basically got kicked out by security over their PA system. It was an interesting experience though. 🏃 💨

Anyways, after changing location, I only stayed with this cigar for another 3 min. It was not only just plain peppery-spice to me at this point, but it also had a harsh bitter taste. Maybe I was drawing too hard that it burned too fast. Regardless, I did not fully enjoy the flavour of this cigar.

My total active smoke time for this one was about 10 min. I did ended it early, but I just don't think it was as tightly packed as Trinidad Reyes, which is about the same size, but lasted me about 40 min. It was another calm evening where there wasn't much wind in place.

I don't enjoy this particular cigar, but I might give a different Cohiba cigar another try since I feel like the way I smoke it might be incorrect. Until I have that chance, please do consider supporting this website if you enjoy reading these posts. Thank you and have a peaceful day! 💞
