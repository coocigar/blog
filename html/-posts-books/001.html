Book Review: The Cigar Companion

The book that we're looking at today is The Cigar Companion: The Connoisseur's Guide by Anwer Bati. The one that I have here is the 3rd edition.

This is a pretty small, but premium book. It is a hard-cover book with glossy paper print. The book is about 222 pages and it's a very light read. The book consists of three main sections.

Section 1: The Story of Cigars
It started with a brief history of cuban cigars. Then it talked about how a cigar is rolled, the difference between machine-made and hand-made cigars, and construction of a cigar. The section also included a brief introduction of various beginner "how to's" throughout. I found this beginning section very educational and entertaining to read.

Section 2: The Cigar Directory
This section is basically a list of cigars. The author included a lot of pictures here! It is sorted alphabetically by the brand name. In each subsection, it starts with a brief background about the brand, a list of cigar names under the brand, a pic of band, and the author's rating/quick info block. I think the cigar pictures in this section is 1:1 ratio, but the book is too small that some longer cigar got cutoff from the page.

Section 3: The Buying and Storing of Cigars
This section could have maybe joined with the first section. It only has about 10 pages worth of content and I personally think it would've been better if they have a section of beginner "how to's" and include this part there. The first section talked about how to select a cigar and also mentioned cigar box. This section also ended the book quite abruptly. There's no departing words or any sort. It just ended 😕

Overall, I like how this book included lots and LOTS of pictures. It's a pretty easy read and I enjoy reading this book overall. It would be a 10 out of 10 book if it was organized better with the content. Nonetheless, I did like the cigar directory part and enjoy looking at different pics of cigars throughout this book!
